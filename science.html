What is Science

  • Science is a system of accumulating knowledge that uses observation and experimentation to describe natural phenomena.
  • It is an accumulated body of knowledge that humanity has gained over the years using that system.
  • Science refers to any systematic field of study of the knowledge gained from it.

History of science

The term science is a Latin word for knowledge: scientia.
What we today refer to as science was until the 1840s called natural philosophy or pre-modern science.
For a true understanding of where we are in science, one needs to look at what happened in the past. The history of science can teach us many lessons about how science should and should not be practiced.
It can also help us understand the direction in which science is heading today.
Without historical perspective, however, you will not fully appreciate what sciences is.

Aspect Of Science

Science is a vast and diverse field with numerous aspects, branches, and sub-disciplines.
Here are some major branches:

  • Physics: Study of matter, energy, and the fundamental forces of nature.
  • Chemistry: Concerned with the composition, structure, and properties of matter.
  • Biology: Study of living organisms and their interactions with each other and their environments.
  • Earth Science: Focuses on the Earth and its processes, including geology, meteorology, and oceanography.
  • Astronomy: Examines celestial objects such as stars, planets, and galaxies.
  • Environmental Science: Investigates the interactions between humans and the environment.
  • Psychology: Studies human behavior and mental processes.
  • Sociology: Analyzes human society, social behavior, and social institutions.
  • Economics: Examines the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services.
  • Political Science: Focuses on political systems, theories, and political behavior.
